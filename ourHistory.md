For 20 years, building tools that solve everyday problems for development teams has 
been the driving force behind MSkold AB. From shipping extensions under the 
Microsoft DevLabs brand — accumulating over 130,000 installs across five extensions 
— to becoming an independent publisher serving more than 30,000 organizations 
worldwide, we have spent two decades earning our place as a trusted name in the 
Azure DevOps marketplace.

This is the history that formed us.

## 2005–2010 – The Early Years
Consulting on Team Foundation Server, the first version of Azure DevOps.
Identifying problems and building small open source utilities, reports and 
Visual Studio extensions.

## 2010–2014 – The Community Years
Taking off in 2010, we joined ALM Rangers projects, contributing to guidance 
materials including the Branching Guide, Test Manager Guide, and various tools.

## 2014–2016 – Azure DevOps Extensibility
Working with the Rangers and Microsoft to provide feedback on extension frameworks 
and the marketplace. Building several extensions released under the Microsoft 
DevLabs brand:
- Work Item Visualization — 70,000+ installs
- Offline Test Execution — 9,000+ installs
- Enhanced Export — 17,000+ installs
- Test Case Explorer — 26,000+ installs
- Sample Data Widget — 6,000+ installs

## 2016 – Enhanced Export PRO: The First Azure DevOps Commercial Extension
As the number of extensions and users grew, we started to get requests for 
prioritised support, custom development, and support contracts. This was 
especially true for Enhanced Export, which had grown fast since its Marketplace 
launch in 2015. The Enhanced Export extension was originally built as a Proof of 
Concept to prove that you could build an Azure DevOps extension without using any 
Microsoft tooling. To meet the demand for new features and provide professional 
support, we restarted development and built Enhanced Export PRO.

We also partnered with the Microsoft Marketplace team to help test and develop the 
marketplace experience for commercial publishers, testing out paid billing through 
Microsoft. That resulted in the go-live of Enhanced Export PRO as a paid extension 
in the Azure Marketplace.

## 2017 – Query Tile PRO
In 2017 Microsoft approached us with an idea for an extension, as there was a need 
for a more advanced dashboard widget to help customers build the dashboards they 
needed. We set out to build our second paid extension based on the feature requests 
Microsoft channelled through to us. In December 2017 we launched Query Tile PRO as 
a paid preview extension.

## 2018–2019 – Microsoft Closes the Commercial Marketplace
In 2018, Microsoft purchased GitHub. That same year they decided to cut support for 
the commercial Paid through Microsoft program. We were then faced with a decision to 
either build our own subscription and billing solution or drop out of the marketplace 
altogether. After careful consideration, we started designing and building our own 
platform, which took most of our available time. The time we didn't spend building 
our subscription and billing platform went to conversations and attempts to reach our 
existing customer base — a challenge made harder by the fact that all communication 
had been handled through Microsoft, leaving us without a customer database.

## 2019–2020 – A New Start
When Microsoft closed its commercial marketplace program on 30 June 2019, we lost 
roughly 75% of our customers overnight. It was a hard reset — but also the beginning 
of building something entirely on our own terms. Our customer base grew back quickly, 
and by the end of 2020 we had recovered around 70% of our customers and were 
continuing to grow at the same rate as before.

## 2020–2022 – Watching, Learning, Preparing
Since Microsoft's merger with GitHub, market uncertainty about the future of Azure 
DevOps made it hard to justify any larger investment. We spent time and energy 
keeping up with the latest developments, looking at the GitHub ecosystem and what 
Microsoft intended to do with it — particularly with regard to extensions for GitHub.

The main focus was to provide first class support and adding features for our existing extensions, 
mainly Enhanced Export PRO.

## 2022–2023 – Query Tile PRO Goes Freemium
In 2022 we had gathered quite a backlog of feature requests and ideas for Query Tile 
PRO. To get them moving, we decided to switch Query Tile PRO from a "free during 
preview" model to a freemium model, where the existing features would remain free 
and the new features we were building would be available to paying customers as 
premium features.

In June 2023 we re-launched Query Tile PRO as a freemium extension and quickly 
built up a solid customer base for the paid premium features.

## 2024 – The Idea Behind Wiki PRO Is Born
During the preceding few years we had increasingly encountered customer frustration 
with Azure DevOps Wiki and the lack of development around it. At the same time, 
customers moving away from Azure DevOps Wiki to other solutions rarely found it to 
be a success story for their development teams.

This gave birth to an idea: what if we could build a Wiki solution that works for 
both stakeholders and software teams?
In 2025 we started the first spike to determine the feasability of building a Wik extenson, and in mid 2025 - we started development of the new Wiki PRO extension.
